/S/C/G NEWS: 8DAYS to bring peace wordwide

We are very proud today to announce our new video game 8DAYS TO BRING PEACE WORLDWIDE

8DAYS is a frenetic local versus action game in which you have the mission to bring peace to the world.

There is an evil power conspiring in the shadows against people's freedom. Choose your hero from a variety of funny characters and travel through multiple locations around the world. You are bounded by duty to bring peace to the world and unmask the evil masterplan. As usual with more violence!

Stay tuned for more info, 8DAYS will start it's early access campaign very soon!

Santa Clara Games at Gamelab 2014

[caption id="attachment_1119" align="aligncenter" width="640"] We were happy to get to gamelab, we didn't know there was a 2 hours waiting line to enter :([/caption]

On June 25 we assisted to Barcelona to Gamelab. We had three intense days of conferences, networking and conversations with friends and other indie developers from Spain. And, as you will see by the pictures, we had chance to talk about HassleHeart with everybody ;)

[caption id="attachment_1154" align="aligncenter" width="640"] First day, first encounter, Mr. Shuhei Yoshida. Great start![/caption]

We met Mr. Shuhei Yoshida(@yosp) (President of Sony's Worldwide Studios) and chat about indie developers like us and the possibility to bring HassleHeart to PSVita (fingers crossed!).

Mr. Keiji Inafune got his own copy of the game too and he seemed to like the idea of a robot tearing apart humans hearts (maybe we gave him ideas for Mighty N.9, who knows...).

Tim Schafer's panel Dreams, machines and inspiration from Gamelab Conference on Vimeo.
We have to apologize withTim Schafer @TimOfLegend whom we boarded after his panel (if you read this, sorry Tim, we hope you will like the game) in order to give him a copy of the game.

We also met the adorable and kind Kim Swift (@K2theSwift) from Amazon Games Studio and had the opportunity to chat with Richard Lemarchand (@rich_lem) who as very nice with us.

[caption id="attachment_1158" align="aligncenter" width="640"] We met Keiji Inafune just before his panel and gave him a HassleHeart copy[/caption]

They all got their own HassleHeart copy with a bonus track, a copy of Return to Castle Urgullas well!

We specially enjoyed Neil McFarland's @parishair panel from UsTwo Games about Monument Valley's development. Their vision about development is similar than ours, and we liked the way they focus on bringing a good experience to the gamers and be honest with them.

[caption id="attachment_1160" align="aligncenter" width="640"] Rami was super kind and shared with us some tricks about indie development[/caption]

On the second day we had the pleasure of lunch with Rami Ismail (@tha_rami) from Vlambeershare ideas and concerns about indie development. He is an awesome and great guy.

CONCEPTOS BÁSICOS PARA ESTUDIOS INDIES INCOMPLETOS Y DESORGANIZADOS from Gamelab Conference on Vimeo.

We feel so sorry we couldn't stay to Rami's inspiring panel, but we ran late to take the airplane back home :,(. Next time, we will try to have more light schedule.

[caption id="attachment_1162" align="aligncenter" width="640"] MakPolloh got his HassleHeart copy too ;)[/caption]

We gone for dinner with our friend Solans (@Hek444) and The Great MakPolloh @MakPolloh from ChecarEsMien.org , who made this funny HassleHear gameplay promised to write a post about Gamelab, Santa Clara Games story and background, indie development and more... we are still waiting man!

[caption id="attachment_1164" align="aligncenter" width="640"] Sharing some beers with MakPollo and Solans at Marsella (where Woody Allen's Vicky, Cristina, Barcelona film was shot)[/caption]

Truth is it's been and incredible experience. People in this industry was very close, friendly and excited about sharing knowledge and experiences. We have learn a lot, powered up our batteries and we are looking forward to make amazing games and show them to everybody next year at Gamelab 2015 !

[caption id="attachment_1166" align="aligncenter" width="640"] We dream of this because we think PSVita is perfect for HassleHeart[/caption]
